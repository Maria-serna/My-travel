LAPLAND - FINLAND

It is a territory of the Nordic countries that is divided between Norway, Sweden, Finland and Russia, most of it above the Arctic Circle. It is the name given to the region where the Lapps (the Sami people) lived and moved around, being in the past a nomadic people.

One of the most particular characteristics of Lapland is the natural phenomenon in which the sun is seen 24 hours a day in summer, or in whic the sun does not rise all day, and therefore, they live an "eternal" night and are able to observe the Northern Lights.

History

The name Lapland could mean "Northern Desert" or "Wild North". The word itself is derived from the ancient Swedish: Lapp or from the origins of Finnish: Lappalainen. Although the true origin and meaning of this word is an enigma since it is not known how the word Lapp appeared in the Nordic languages.

It’s widely believed that Lapland was first inhabited after the Scandinavian ice caps began to melt around 7000 years ago. The Sami people were among the first natives of the region and rock carvings from thousands of years ago. Like many parts of the world, wars and disputes have had massive impact on the land and its people.

The Sami are the only indigenous people in the European Union and reside in the northern regions of Finland, Norway and Sweden, as well as in parts of northeastern Russia. In Finland, the Sami population is approximately 6000 and the preservation of their language and culture, which is in danger of extinction, is governed by the autonomous parliament of Inari, Finland.

Place to visit

Northern Lights Ranch - Kittilä

Is a unique complex restaurant, accommodation and activity facility located in Finnish Lapland, 15 km from Levi Ski Resort and 30 km from Kittilä airport. Each Sky View Cabin is designed with huge glass windows and heated glass roofs making them ideal for viewing the millions of stars are lit in the sky and very often the Northern Lights appear in the sky as well.

Levi Ski Resort - Levi

Levi is the biggest ski resort in Finland with the pistes available for downhill skiers and snowboarders, beginners or advanced skiers as well as children. There are altogether 43 pistes with 27 lifts. For those who need coaching Levi has an international Ski School. In addition, summer and autumn activities such as disc golfing, downhill cycling, mini-golf and summer sledding take place in the Levi Activity Park.

Lapinkylä – Levi

Here you can find the reindeer farm, you can feed them and share with them. You can also enjoy a reindeer ride on different trails in the forest. The journey takes 30 to 50 minutes. Along the way, you can enjoy incredible landscapes and, on the way, back to the reindeer farm, you can enjoy donut coffees in warm white whiskers.

Santa Claus Village - Rovaniemi

Santa Claus Village is situated 8 km to the north of Rovaniemi. Here you will find the office of Santa Claus, a restaurant and ice-cream hotel, souvenir shops, restaurants and several activities such as sleigh rides pulled by reindeer, snowmobile, dog sled rides. You can meet Santa Claus and cross the magical Arctic Circle

Restaurants

Saamen Kammi

Located in the courtyard of the hotel is an authentic Sámi turf gammi or hut built into the ground. With its unique atmosphere, the Saamen Kammi offers traditional Lapland delicacies by an open fire. Exotic joikha music and tales of Lapland accompany dinner and supper in the restaurant.

Menu includes:

Clear fish soup of whitefish and cold smoked trout/ Smoked reindeer steak, Rowanberry-game sauce/ Sautéed reindeer/ Reindeer rib’s/ Kammi's host's stew/ Glow-fried salmon/ Mashed potatoes/ Herb flavoured potatoes/ Winter vegetables/ Forest mushroom sauce. Dessert: Lappish cheese and cloudberries

Snowman World Ice Restaurant

The world’s only ice restaurant located exactly on the Arctic Circle, Santa Claus’ home turf - Rovaniemi. Incredibly ice sculptures, exciting details and a menu created from local ingredients.

Snowman World Ice Restaurant serves local lunch: salmon soup with house bread complemented with arctic berry dessert as well as coffee and tea.

Who would you visit this wonderful place with?

I would like to take this trip with my best friend. It has always been my dream to be able to observe the northern lights and this trip could be a great adventure from which we will have beautiful memories.

Travel Itinerary

Day O:Take a flight from Jose Maria Cordoba airport in Rionegro with a few stopovers to Kittilä airport (approximately 22 hours).

Day 1:We will arrive in the morning. Levi is located only 15 minutes drive from Kittilä airport. The hotel we have chosen for the accommodation (northern lights ranch) is situated approximately 28 minutes from the Kittilä airport. When we arrive at the hotel, we will have something to eat and rest, and in the afternoon, we will take the opportunity to explore the surroundings and enjoy the sauna. In the evening we will have dinner and stay inside the hotel trying to observe the northern lights. These can only be seen from September to April, the best time being between December and January.

Day 2:We will have breakfast and then leave for the Levi Ski Resort, where we will take ski lessons at the International Ski School and skate all morning. Afterwards we will have lunch and rent snowmobiles to enjoy a guided tour of the landscapes and if we have the opportunity, we will take the opportunity to learn how to ice fish. In the evening we will go to the village of Levi to taste a delicious hot chocolate together with gingerbread cookies and strawberries from the forest. In the evening we will return to the hotel, where a delicious Finnish style dinner will be waiting for us.

Day 3:We will get up early, have breakfast and then drive to Lapinkylä. Here we will share with the reindeer, feed them and take many pictures. In addition, we will take a reindeer ride through the forest and on the way back to this reindeer farm we will enjoy a hot drink and the delights of the restaurant.

In the evening we will go to the Saamen Kammi restaurant, have dinner with one of the typical dishes and learn a little about the culture of this beautiful indigenous European population. We will return to the hotel and prepare to go in search of the Northern Lights.

Day 4:We will get up early to go to Rovaniemi. The drive takes about 2 hours and 30 minutes. We will arrive at approximately 10 am. In this city, which is the capital of Lapland, we will enjoy Santa Claus Village. For lunch, we will visit the Snowman World Ice Restaurant. In the afternoon, we will tour Santa Klaus Village and cross the Arctic Circle. In the evening we will explore the nightlife of Rovaniemi and go to rest in a hotel.

Day 5:In the morning we will leave for the airport of Rovanemi and take a flight back to the airport Jose Maria Cordoba, Rionegro.

LAPLAND

The island of Aruba is an autonomous island country located in the southern Caribbean, 29 kilometers northwest of Venezuela. Until 1986, it was part of the Netherlands Antilles. The languages spoken on the island are Papiamento, Dutch, English and Spanish. The island is only about 20 miles long and 6 miles wide.

In addition to great weather, Aruba also boasts beautiful resorts, casinos and gorgeous beaches. Aruba is famous for its pristine beaches, you can diving, crossing the island in a 4 × 4 or diving into the Atlantis VI submarine and observing the beautiful coral reefs.


History

The first people to inhabit the island were the Arawak Indians. The name Aruba appears to have been derived from the Indian word Arawak oibubai, which means guide. In 1499, the Spanish explorer Alonso de Ojeda claimed the territory for the Spanish crown. Almost 200 years later, the Dutch captured the islands of Aruba, Curaçao and Bonaire from the Spanish.

Place to visit

Palm Beach

It is a strip two kilometers long, home to glamorous hotels, perfect for practicing water sports. Palm Beach is where you can find the majority of Aruba’s high-rise resorts. This area also boasts many restaurants, casinos, bars and shops and offers some of the best nightlife in Aruba.

Eagle Beach

It is the widest beach in Aruba and famous for its soft white sand. Eagle Beach features picnic areas, cabins, beach tennis courts and motorized water sports sites. This beach is also home to Aruba's flagship tree, the Divi Divi, which always grows crooked in strong winds.

Arikok National Park

Covers approximately 20 percent of the island. The park is also home to three beaches: Dos Playa, Boca Prins and Daimari Beach. One of the park’s most popular features is the Natural Pool. It is better to explore this area in 4×4 vehicles or on horseback.

Baby beach

You can find this beach at the southeast end of the island; this is one of the less crowded beaches and is a place where you can regularly see sea turtles. Baby Beach is also popular with kite and windsurfers and you can find the best snorkeling.
















































Oranjestad

Is the biggest city in Aruba and also its capital. It is also a popular cruise ship port. This is a place tx free shopping. Strolling through Oranjestad, you’ll find a mixture of colorful colonial buildings and high-rise resorts, restaurants, bars and shops. The main location for shopping in Oranjestad is Caya GF Betico Croes. You will find jewelers and clothing stores of prestigious designers.

Restaurants

The restaurant options in Aruba are varied, you can find traditional Dutch, Asian, African and Spanish cuisine.

Faro blanco

Faro Blanco Restaurant brings the best of Italy’s cuisine to Aruba. Traditional Italian recipes paired with over 100 wine selections from around the world. Tucked away at Aruba’s most northern coast, atop of the hill of Hudishibana at the foot of the California Lighthouse Landmark.

Pinchos Grill & Bar

It's an informal but elegant grill and bar on a dock completely above the crystal clear blue waters of Aruba. The restaurant's name comes from the Spanish word for a skewered sandwich, so there are always a few of those on the menu.

Driftwood Restaurant

It is an authentic and local restaurant, located in the center of Oranjestad. The name "Driftwood" is due to the rustic interior of the bar and dining room, built by hand with natural driftwood. The menu includes a wide variety of typical Aruban delicacies, including three fish soups and fish fillets with a mild Aruban Creole sauce.
